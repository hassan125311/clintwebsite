Agriculture is the art and science of cultivating the soil, growing crops, and raising livestock. It includes the preparation of plant and animal products for people to use and their distribution to markets. Agriculture provides most of the world’s food and fabrics. Cotton, wool, and leather are all agricultural products. Agriculture also provides wood for construction and paper products.

Key Definitions in Agriculture

Agriculture:

The practice of farming, including the cultivation of the soil for the growing of crops and the rearing of animals to provide food, wool, and other products.

Crop Rotation:

The practice of growing different types of crops in the same area in sequential seasons to maintain the fertility of the soil.

Irrigation:

The artificial application of water to the soil, usually to assist in growing crops.

Livestock:

Domesticated animals raised in an agricultural setting to produce commodities such as food, fiber, and labor.
